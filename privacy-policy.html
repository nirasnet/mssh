Privacy Policy

Our Commitment

We believe your privacy is fundamental. mSSH is designed with privacy-first principles: we do not collect analytics, do not track your activity, do not use third-party SDKs for data collection, and do not share your data with anyone.

1. Data We Collect

Information You Provide

When you add SSH connection profiles to mSSH, you provide:

Connection Details Host address, port number, and username for SSH servers you connect to
Authentication Credentials SSH passwords and private keys (optional, for authentication)
Connection Profiles Named profiles you create to organize and manage your SSH connections

Automatically Collected Information

Device-level biometric data: If you enable Face ID for app lock, iOS's Face ID framework processes your biometric data. This data never leaves your device and is managed entirely by iOS.

Host key fingerprints: When you connect to SSH servers, the server's host key is stored locally on your device for security verification (Trust On First Use).

What We Do NOT Collect

  • Analytics or usage data
  • Tracking information or user identifiers
  • Device information (UDID, Apple ID, etc.)
  • Location data
  • Browsing history or app activity logs
  • Personal identifiers beyond what you explicitly enter
  • Contents of SSH terminals or command history

2. How We Use Your Data

Primary Purpose

All data you provide is used solely to enable the core functionality of mSSH:

  • Establishing SSH connections to remote servers
  • Storing connection profiles for your convenience
  • Verifying server authenticity via host key validation
  • Providing biometric app lock via Face ID

No Secondary Uses

We do not use your data for:

  • Marketing or promotional purposes
  • Building user profiles or analytics
  • Improving algorithms or machine learning
  • Selling, renting, or sharing with third parties
  • Any purpose beyond providing the App functionality

3. Data Storage & Security

Local Device Storage

All your data is stored exclusively on your iOS device using Apple's native frameworks:

Connection Profiles (Host, Port, Username) Stored in SwiftData, a local database encrypted by iOS file system
SSH Passwords & Private Keys Stored in iOS Keychain with device-only protection
Known Host Keys Stored in iOS Keychain and local SwiftData database
Face ID Data Managed by iOS's secure enclave; never accessible to mSSH

Encryption Standards

  • In Transit: All SSH connections use standard SSH2 protocol with end-to-end encryption. Data is encrypted from your device to the remote server.
  • At Rest: Sensitive credentials are protected by iOS Keychain's `kSecAttrAccessibleWhenUnlockedThisDeviceOnly` policy—they are only accessible when the device is unlocked.
  • Device Storage: iOS encrypts all app data using FileVault and device-level encryption.

No Server-Side Storage

mSSH does not maintain any servers, databases, or cloud infrastructure (except optional iCloud sync—see below). Your data is yours alone.

4. iCloud Sync (Optional)

Enabling iCloud Sync

mSSH offers optional iCloud synchronization for your connection profiles. This feature is disabled by default.

What Gets Synced

Connection profiles only:

  • Host address, port, and username

What Does NOT Get Synced

Never shared with iCloud:

  • SSH passwords
  • Private SSH keys
  • Keychain credentials
  • Any sensitive authentication data

CloudKit Privacy

iCloud sync uses Apple CloudKit with the following settings:

  • CloudKit database is configured with `cloudKitDatabase: .none` to exclude sensitive profiles if desired
  • Your iCloud account is the only entity with access to your synced data
  • Apple's privacy commitments apply: apple.com/privacy

Disabling iCloud Sync

You can disable iCloud sync at any time in the App settings. Disabling sync does not delete data from your device.

5. Third-Party Sharing

We Do Not Share Your Data

mSSH does not sell, rent, trade, or share any of your personal data with third parties, including:

  • Analytics providers
  • Advertising networks
  • Data brokers
  • Marketing companies
  • Service providers or contractors

No Third-Party SDKs

mSSH does not integrate any third-party SDKs for analytics, tracking, or data collection. The App uses only open-source libraries for SSH protocol support (Citadel) and terminal emulation (SwiftTerm), neither of which collect user data.

Legal Compliance Exceptions

We may disclose your data if required by law (e.g., court order, government request), but we will not proactively volunteer information and will comply with applicable law.

6. GDPR & CCPA Compliance

GDPR (European Users)

If you are located in the European Union or European Economic Area, the following rights apply under GDPR:

Right of Access You can request a copy of your data stored on your device at any time
Right to Erasure You can delete all data by uninstalling the App or clearing App data in Settings
Right to Portability Your connection profiles are stored in standard formats and can be exported
Right to Object You can disable iCloud sync at any time or refuse biometric unlock

Legal Basis for Processing: All data processing is based on performance of contract (providing SSH functionality) and your explicit consent.

CCPA (California Residents)

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under CCPA:

  • Right to Know: You have the right to know what personal information is collected. See "Data We Collect" above.
  • Right to Delete: You can delete your data by uninstalling the App or clearing its data in iOS Settings.
  • Right to Opt-Out: mSSH does not sell personal information, so this right does not apply.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We do not discriminate based on privacy choices.

How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at tmtgwdx@gmail.com with details of your request.

7. Data Deletion

Deleting Your Data

You have complete control over your data. To delete all data stored by mSSH:

  1. On your iOS device, go to Settings → General → iPhone Storage
  2. Find and select mSSH
  3. Tap Offload App (keeps data temporarily) or Delete App (removes everything)

iCloud Data

If you have iCloud sync enabled, deleting the App does not automatically delete synced connection profiles from iCloud. You can manually delete them via Settings → [Your Name] → iCloud → Manage Storage.

8. Security Practices

App Security

  • mSSH uses industry-standard SSH2 encryption for all remote connections
  • Private keys are protected by iOS Keychain with device-only access policies
  • Host key verification (TOFU) prevents man-in-the-middle attacks
  • Optional Face ID lock adds biometric protection to the App

Our Responsibility

We are responsible for protecting data stored within mSSH. However, the security of your remote SSH servers and credentials depends on how you manage them. We recommend:

  • Using strong, unique SSH key passphrases
  • Keeping your iOS device updated with the latest security patches
  • Using device encryption (standard on modern iOS)
  • Enabling Face ID or passcode lock for the App

No Guarantees

While we implement reasonable security measures, no system is 100% secure. mSSH is provided "as is" without warranty of absolute security.

This App is a terminal utility designed for technical users and developers. It is not appropriate for children.
